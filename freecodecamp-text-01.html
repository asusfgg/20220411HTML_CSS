Tribute to Academician Yuan Longping

The founder of China's hybrid rice business.

Here's a time line of Academician Yuan Longping's life:

  • September 7, 1930 -- Yuan Longping was born in Peking Union Medical College Hospital.
  • February 1948 to April 1949 -- Yuan Longping was a high school student at the High School Affiliated to Nanjing Central University (now the High School Affiliated to Nanjing Normal University ).
  • August 1949 to October 1950 Yuan Longping studied at the Department of Agriculture of Xianghui College in Xiaba, Beibei, Chongqing.
  • November 1950 to July 1953 the faculties were adjusted and merged into the Department of Agriculture of Southwest Agricultural College, which was newly built in Chongqing. Yuan Longping continued his studies for 3 years until graduation.
  • July 5, 1964 Yuan Longping found a "natural male sterile plant" in the experimental paddy field. After artificial pollination, hundreds of seeds of the first generation male sterile plant were produced.
  • 1976 hybrid rice was successfully promoted.
  • October 1980 Yuan Longping, the first breeder in my country to study hybrid rice and a researcher at Hunan Academy of Agricultural Sciences, after more than 10 years of hard research and experiments, with the cooperation of relevant scientific research units, overcame the barrier of seed production and enabled the research on hybrid rice. The overall success has opened up a new way for increasing rice production.
  • June 2020 Yuan Longping's team grew rice in the alpine and saline-alkali-tolerant rice (also known as sea rice) that was tested in the saline -alkali land of Qaidam Basin in Qinghai.
  • At 13:07 on May 22, 2021 Yuan Longping passed away in Changsha, Hunan at the age of 91.

"Yuan Longping has been engaged in the research of hybrid rice for half a century. He is not afraid of difficulties, is willing to make sacrifices, works hard and pursues hard, and has made great contributions to solving the problem of food for the Chinese people. His outstanding achievements not only belong to China, but also affect the world."

--- Xinhua Net Review.
